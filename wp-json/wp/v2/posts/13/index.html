Igor Stimac on getting red card for interrupting Pakistan player: \u2018I will do it again when needed to protect our boys\u2019<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Bengaluru:<\/strong> India head coach Igor Stimac on Thursday defended his actions during the team\u2019s 4-0 win over Pakistan in its SAFF Championship opener here, saying he \u201cwill do it again\u201d to protect his players against \u201cunjustified decisions\u201d.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Stimac was shown a red card for interrupting the actions of an opposition player deliberately at the Sree Kanteerava Stadium on Wednesday.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u201cFootball is all about passion, especially when you defend the colours of your country. You can hate or love me for my actions yesterday, but I am a warrior and I will do it again when needed to protect our boys on the pitch against unjustified decisions,\u201d Stimac tweeted.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The rain-drenched first half was meandering to its conclusion with the home side leading 2-0 after two Sunil Chhetri goals. But a moment of indiscretion from Stimac suddenly livened up the proceedings.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Stimac, a vastly experienced coach and player, chose to interfere when Pakistan player Abdullah Iqbal was ready to make a throw-in. Stimac tried to pull back the ball from the player, sparking wild reactions from some visiting players as well as some coaching staff.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The referee Prajwal Chhetri and other match officials had to intervene to separate the intensely sparring individuals.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

As the scuffle was brought under control referee Chhetri showed red card to Stimac, as per the football rules, for interrupting with the actions of an opposition player deliberately.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Stimac could not stand on the sideline for the rest of the match, with former India defending Mahesh Gawli doing duty on the touchline.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

A few players from India and Pakistan teams too were flashed yellow cards for intervening unnecessarily in the episode.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

After rout of Pakistan, India assistant coach Gawli said the red card might appear a bit harsh on his senior, but the referee had to go by the book.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u201cYeah, it was an offence that if you go by the book often attracts a red card. But yeah, but then we feel that it might have been a bit harsh on the coach,\u201d Gawli had said during the post-match press conference.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
